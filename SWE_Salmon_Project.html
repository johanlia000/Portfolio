SALMON FUN RUN

Context: Society of Women Engineerings (SWE) Hackathon 2019


Duration: 9 hours


Collaboration: Member of 4-person team


Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Figma

As part of my team, I ideated, designed, and competed in the Society of Women Engineer's (SWE) hackathon at the University of Washington. This year my team focused on SWE's prompt about "harnessing the potential of indiviudals, technology, and collaboration to help the greater Seattle area." My team focused on educating and helping combat the environmental decline in the puget sound region that directly affects salmon populations.

RESEARCH



My team and I didnâ€™t know a lot about about the declining salmon population in the Puget Sound. As a team, we each spent 20 minutes researching key information about salmon and their environment, why there is a salmon decline, and current solutions.

IDEATION


Based on our research we created our how might we statement that would serve as a design pillar for our ideation stage.


Our goal was to educate the public while also creating a sense of community. Thinking outside of the box, we came up with the idea of a fun run. We knew that people in Seattle are active, outdoorsy people, so we assumed participation rates would be high.

The core goal of the run is to spark passion and conversation in hopes of creating change through creating community. Lastly, we had to think about how to incorporate technology into the fun run. We decided on the education aspect of the run would be facilitated by an app.

DESIGN


After we ideated, it was time to refine our initial idea into a final design. We had to figure out what we wanted to teach the public and how to seamlessly integrate it into the fun run.

I came up with the idea of creating an obstacle course in the run that mimics the obstacles salmon have to face throughout their lives. This would be a run, challenging aspect to the run. These obstacles would be things like dams, fish ladders, temperature change, and swimming upstream. For example, swimming upstream would be represented as running up a hill. I created the obstacle course map to reflect the shape of a fish.


To integrate the education part, we decided there will be frequent checkpoints along the race with scannable QR codes. The participant can scan the QR code with their phone, which takes them to information about that checkpoint, delivered by audio or reading. This allows for fluid usability that connects the participant with the course.

DESIGN


I took lead on designing the app wireframes on Figma. I created two main features of the app: the course map and the information pages that paired with each obstacle. I wanted the interface to be straightforward to use, as user would be in the middle of the fun run when accessing the app.
