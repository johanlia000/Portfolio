The Coeur System

Context: Project for Design for America


Duration: 2 Quarters (Fall 2017 - Winter 2018)


Collaboration: Project Lead of 5-person team


Tools: Adobe Illustrator

As the project lead of my Design for America at Univesrity of Washington (DFA) project, I led a 5-person interdisciplinary team in the design of an original, wearable technology prototype that helps high school students relax during a panic attack at school. Through this project I practiced my leadership, teamwork, and human-centered design skills.

PROBLEM AREA



My group's problem area was high school student stress and anxiety. High school students struggle with the pressure of achieving high standardized test scores and maintaining a 4.0 GPA while taking multiple AP/IB courses in hopes of being accepted to a prestigious college. This is a growing problem in the Seattle area, as massive amounts of pressure and stress negatively affects students’ mental health.

SECONDARY RESEARCH


To learn more about our problem space, we dove into secondary research.

For our secondary research it was important to focus on learning more about the symptoms and definition of anxiety, existing solutions, mindfulness, and academic pressure. This helped us get a solid foundation in order to thoroughly understand our problem area.







Two popular anxiety apps


Aftering researching exisiting solutions in order to help us understand the market, we learned that current anxiety apps revolve around mindfulness and meditation that help people track their mood, offer relaxation and breathing tips, or let you play a relaxing game. This helped target growth areas within the current market.

PRIMARY RESEARCH


For our primary research we worked closely with our community partner, Roosevelt High School, to understand high schooler’s thoughts and feelings regarding their own mental health. Our methods included an online survey, love/hate boxes, and one one-on-one interview with a high school junior. Because mental health is such a sensitive subject, we ensured that our research methods were thoughtful and anonymous.


ONLINE SURVEY

The purpose for our online survey was to get an understanding of high schooler’s feelings towards their own or their friends’ mental health, what makes them stressed, and how they relax. We received 240 responses.



LOVE/HATE BOXES

We chose to implement love/hate boxes in Roosevelt High School’s teen health center. This was used to give a more open ended question to understand how high schoolers feel. We had two boxes, one for love and one for hate. Students who felt comfortable wrote down what objects, activities, and people whom they love and/or hate on an anonymous slip of paper.


ONE-ON-ONE INTERVIEW

Lastly, I conducted a one-on-one interview with a high school junior to learn more about a new mindfulness club at the high school and listen to a student’s perspective about mental health, specifically anxiety.


RESULTS

1) Teens are stressed because they feel like they have to balance school assignments, college applications, and extracurricular activities.


2) Students are self aware and know they are stressed when their behavior is more aggressive and they have difficulty focusing on every day tasks.


3) To relieve their stress high schoolers enjoy hanging out with friends, listening to music, or taking deep breaths.

IDEATION



After the research phase we created our project’s How Might We statement.






Initially, we thought about creating an app that would help high schoolers with their anxiety, but we didn’t want to just create another mindfulness app. After speaking with our design coaches they challenged us to create something new and futureistic

We then created 3 design principles that would be at the core of our ideation phase.





We became interested in the tech area of wearables. We ideated different wearable stress relieving devices: a sticky, thin pad they could wear on their heart, attachable monitor on pant waists, a or modern bracelet.

We ideated utilizing calming blue and green lighting and pulsating motions to calm students during panic attacks. Also, based on our primary research, it was important to incorporate a customizable music aspect to the wearable. But our biggest goal was to create a wearable technology that would give the student a support system they could trust.



Balloon protoype of "breathing" feature


Narrowing down our focus we ideated futuristic in-classroom devices. We then concluded in creating a wearable technology that would be implemented in a classroom environment. We used sewing, cardboard, balloons, and 3D printing to prototype.

FINAL DESIGN




The Coeur system is an entire network that utilizes a high-tech stress ball to create community support for students dealing with panic attacks. There are two components to the system: the stress ball and docking station.



User Story


At school, a student is able to discreetly carry around the stress ball that will “breathe” with them during a panic attack through gentle expansion and compression. Tha Coeur System will be connected to the student's chosen support system such as educators, nurses, and parents through an app. The ball will alert the student's support system when a student is experiencing a panic attack with customizable vibrations.

At home, the Coeur Ball will rest on its wireless docking station that illuminates calming blue and green light. It will also serve as a speaker that can play music to help with generalized anxiety.

To showcase our work and project we presented at Artefact to community members, professors, and Artefact employees at our final expo.

OUR TEAM






Our interdisciplinary team consisted of Human Centered Design and Engineering, business, electricial engineering, and industrial design majors ranging from freshman to seniors. Our whole team is passionate about design, technology, and designing for social good. Each individual brought different persepctives and unique backgrounds to the design process.

TAKEAWAYS



Create the future

I learned that physical software/hardware limitations should not be a limitation for design. My team could have just created another app, but with some encouraging we designed something that is futuristic and new. New technology isn't anything without design.


Teamwork includes friendship

For me, teamwork includes friendship. My team bonded over late night udon and funny pet stories. Our team bonding created a team dynamic that was welcoming and safe for creative, outside of the box ideas, which helped our project be its best.
