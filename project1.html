FARESTART BRAND BOOK

This was my final project for my class HCDE 308: Visual Communication in Human Centered Design and Engineering. I played the part as design consultant for my client, a non-profit: FareStart. I used a 5-design phase to create a cohesive visual system that represented my clients ethos and mission.

PROJECT DETAILS

DESIGN RESEARCH

This was the foundation of my brand book project. I conducted background research on my client company to understand their mission and how that mission is visually represented in their current brand identity and how it can it can be improved. This included researching their audiences, types of relationships they have with their audiences, and how their mission is visually seen through their web presence and physical locations.

View Design Research Presentation: Research.

 

 

LOGO DESIGN

For FareStart's logo I wanted to focus on FareStart helping individuals grow by teaching them life and work skills. I showed this personal growth with leaves blooming from the spoon. The spoon represents FareStart's main focus, food, and through their culinary programs and apprentiships, FareStart teaches and gives its participants a second chance.

MOBILE WIREFRAMES

I started this aspect with viewing their current website and pin pointing pain points for the user. I targeted the issue with their catering form did not allow for user specifications. They have multiple menus for catering, one specific to weddings, so I chose to create a form specifically for users who need wedding catering.

BRAND BOOK

This was the last phase of my project. My brand book is a culmination of my design work for rebranding FareStart. It was where I could put together a story about FareStart and who they are through their visual presence. This includes visual about their mission, logo, icons, color palette, photo choices that all reflect FareStart's mission and goals.
