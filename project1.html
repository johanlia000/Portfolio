FARESTART BRAND BOOK

Context: Final project for HCDE 308 (Visual Communication in Human Centered Design and Engineering)


Duration: 3 weeks (Winter 2019)


Collaboration: Individual work


Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe XD

As part of this project I played the role of a design consultant for my client, FareStart. FareStart is a nonprofit organization that focuses on providing real solutions to poverty and homelessness through food and food service. My job was to create a cohesive visual system that represented the client's ethos and mission.

After practicing design principles, typographic hierarchy, color, and integration of visual elements I used a 5-design phase to create a new visual system for FareStart that communicated inclusiveness and excellence. This included researching their mission, redesigning their logo, creating icons and mobile interfaces, and combining those elements into a brand book.

DESIGN RESEARCH

This was the foundation of my rebranding project. For this part I worked with a team of 4 to conduct backgorund research on FareStart, the client company, to understand their mission and how that mission is currently visually represented in their brand identity and how it can it can be improved.

First, we researched their audiences and relationships. This helped get an understanding on who our visuals would reach and connect with. Then we did a brand identity analysis where we looked into their mission statement and engagement they promote and analyze of how it was successfully or unsuccessfully portrayed in their brand identity.





Our last step was to analyze their social media (Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter) and website to find inconsistencies with their mission and confusing and unsuccessful design aspects. All of this design research helped me redesign their visual brand identity to successfully portray their mission and reach their targeted audiences.

View the design research presentation here.

LOGO REDESIGN



The original logo is very simple and I liked the warm tone orange that reflects their mission of compassion in helping people. But it gave off a soup kitchen vibe, which did not reflect their goal of presenting themselves as a high class restaurant.





I started the redesign of the logo by brainstorming FareStart's mission and goals and then the inportant parts of their mission I wanted to include in their logo. Instead of focusing on the food aspect of FareStart, I wanted to hone in on their unique mission of helping individuals grow by teaching them new life and work skills through food. I show the personal growth with leaves blooming from the spoon; the spoon representing FareStart teaching individuals skills through their culinary programs and apprenticeships. I chose to stick with the orange color because it creates a sense of warmth and comfort to resemble FareStart's mission of caring and helping others.



ICON DESIGN

Before designing the icons, I brainstormed important features on FareStart's website. I concluded that the two most important actions were signing up for catering and looking at their menus.

Menu Icon.

Catering Icon.

The menu icon leads the user to the list of FareStart's different menus, and the catering icon leads the user to its catering form to fill out. The icons are the same color scheme as the logo to create cohesion across the visuals. I also wanted to match the rounded corners and boldness of the spoon in the logo with the icons to convey a sense of friendliness and warmth.

MOBILE WIREFRAMES

I created three wireframes to show the micro interaction of choosing a menu item to taste during a wedding catering meeting with FareStart.



MOBILE WIREFRAMES

I started this aspect with viewing their current website and pin pointing pain points for the user. I targeted the issue with their catering form did not allow for user specifications. They have multiple menus for catering, one specific to weddings, so I chose to create a form specifically for users who need wedding catering.

BRAND BOOK

This was the last phase of my project. My brand book is a culmination of my design work for rebranding FareStart. It was where I could put together a story about FareStart and who they are through their visual presence. This includes visual about their mission, logo, icons, color palette, photo choices that all reflect FareStart's mission and goals.
