This was our final project in my INFO 201 class where I combined all of the skills I learned throughout the course and stretch my skills by diving into an interesting dataset and creating an interactive web application.

DATASET

My team and I were all sophomores so college admissions were still fresh in our minds. We knew we wanted to make an app that would help graduating high school students apply/choose a college. So, the primary audiences were high school and college transfer studnets wanting to apply to college, and the secondary audience were the applicants' parents. We narrowed down our dataset based on key information studnets would want to know such as location, diversity, test socres, and admission statistics.

The dataset we used is from the U.S Department of Education. It is from the most recent years (past 20 years) showing admission rates, tuition rates, SAT/ACT scores, and more. Click here to see the data. We attained the data in R through reading the downloaded CSV file.

When applying to schools, students want to know the average accepted admissions ACT/SAT scores. I decided to create bar charts that would show the comparison in scores from cumulative, math, writing, and reading sections in the standarized tests from a chosen school.







The user is prompted to select a state and college from two drop down menus. It then shows one bar chart for ACT scores and one for SAT scores as some students take the ACT and others take the SAT. This allows all students to look at the college's admission scores and compare with what they got or should strive towards.

SHINY APP

We created four interactive widgets that showed the data through visualizations.

1. Location: The locations tab allows you locate all the available colleges within a selected state. Hover your mouse over a marker to get the full name of the college.

2. Diversity: The diversity tab allows you to look at race data for colleges. Simply choose a state that you're interested in and then choose a college within that state. A pie chart should show up that gives the percentages of each race. You can hover over the pie chart slices and see the exact percentages if it is hard to read. A table also renders giving the same data on race percentages to allow the user to see the data in a graph and table view.

3. Score: The score tab allows students to look at the median/middle scores (both ACT and SAT) for a particular school. Choose a state and then choose a college/university that is in that state from the drop down menu. A bar graph will show the midpoint ACT and another bar graph will show the midpoint SAT scores. For the ACT this includes midpoint of cumulative, english, math, and writing scores. For the SAT this includes average, math, reading, and writing scores.

4. Admission Statistics: The admissions tab allows students to look at their selected colleges' admission rates and compare them to one another. Furthermore, students can also look at a table of information with the name, city, state, instate tuition, outstate tuition, and the college's website.
