My ONLINE PORTFOLIO

Context: Show my design and programming skills through my website portfolio


Duration: Ongoing


Collaboration: Individual


Tools: HTML, CSS, Adobe Illustrator

I wanted to take the challenge as a UX designer and programmer to learn, create, and code my own portfolio to show off my design and programming skills. This is an ongoing project of designing, learning, improving and coding.

GOALS



My main goal of creating and coding this website portfolio was to be able to show both my programming and design skills combined. This strategy seemed like a fun, meta way to show my interests and skills.




I wanted my portfolio to be clean and simple while clearly communicating each project's development and process story. This included creating strong organizational grids and maintaining consistency across all projects.




Visuals and color were important aspects that I wanted to incorporate. Visuals are able to tell the story of the design and its process while color engages the reader.

IDEATION


When thinking about my online portfolio I looked at inspiration and knew that I wanted to keep it clean and simple while still telling the story of my projects.

I am interested in both design and computer science, and I wanted to be able to show both types of projects on my portfolio.

The three components to this website is the homepage, my projects (both programming and design), and contact me.





THE HOMEPAGE






In this home/about me page, I wanted it to hold two pieces of information: about me concerning my education and work aspirations and about me concerning my interests outside of work.





My PROJECT PAGE(S)









On the design and programming pages I wanted to visually show each individual project I have done by creating clickable squares with photos that represent each project. These photos are meant to interest the reader.

After clicking on a project, you can read more about that specific project. Across all projects, both design and programming, I wanted to clearly state the project overview in a clear manner; this information is clearly presented at the top of each project's page. After the overview, the project details would be broken up into smaller, digestable chunks of information that are accompanied by project photos.

I also wanted to share my key takeaways from each project, because all of my projects are important in my career and personal goals and growth. For each takeaway my goal is to make an icon (on Illustrator) representing that takeaway.

CODING



I wanted to learn how to create a website. I started with a website template that mimicked what I wanted my website to look like and then altered the grid lines, photos, font sizes, and colors. I have self taught myself HTML and CSS through Googling and CodecAdemy. I still have a lot to learn (such as formatting my website for phones & tablets), but it's an ongoing project that I constantly want to improve.

TAKEAWAYS




Take bite size pieces

When I decided to create my own portfolio website, I felt overwhelmed. I knew that I had to learn a new coding language, design the layout to fit my vision, and write up the project stories. To compat this overwhelming feeling, I chunked the project into smaller tasks and spread them out overtime. This helped me keep organized and on track.


Learning is applying

Learning anything new is challenging. I have a strong background in Java through robotics, programming courses, and internships, which helps me learn new languages. But I grow and learn the most when I'm applying the content to real life projects, like this portfolio.
